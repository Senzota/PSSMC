Chapter 11  ·  PSSMC Mathematics

Future Works

A simulation that wins by 50% on the worst case is a strong start — not a destination. Five directions for the PSSMC are open, each reachable from the foundations built in Chapters 1–10. The longest chapter is the one not yet written.

🔧
Direction 01 · Hardware

Hardware implementation on a real CNC test rig

The next step is to leave SciPy and run the PSSMC on a physical two-DOF chatter rig — ideally a Kasprowiak-style aluminium block instrumented with accelerometers and a piezo-actuator in the feedback loop. The controller would execute on a real-time target (Speedgoat, dSPACE, or a Raspberry-Pi CM5 with PREEMPT-RT).

  • Sampling. Confirm the 10 kHz inner loop is sufficient given measurement noise.
  • Quantisation. Study sensitivity to 12-bit vs 16-bit ADC resolution on \(s(t)\).
  • Actuator dynamics. Add a first-order lag to the model; verify the Lyapunov certificate still holds.
  • Calibration. Tune \(\gamma_1, \gamma_2, \gamma_3\) on the actual machine — the simulated values are a starting point.
🌊
Direction 02 · Disturbance Modelling

Higher-fidelity disturbance models

The current model uses a fixed regenerative-chatter law \(F_{cut} = K_c\, a_p\, (f_z - \Delta y_1)\). In reality, several effects make the disturbance non-stationary: tool wear, thermal expansion, workpiece inhomogeneity, and chip-segmentation dynamics.

  • Tool wear. Model \(K_c\) as a slowly increasing function of cumulative cutting energy.
  • Thermal drift. Add a slow stiffness drift \(k_1(T)\) coupled to a heat-equation surrogate.
  • Chip-segmentation. Replace the linear regenerative term with a piecewise model that captures the saw-tooth chip morphology of titanium-class materials.
  • Sensor noise. Coloured (1/f) accelerometer noise rather than white — re-check robustness.
🧠
Direction 03 · Machine Learning

Machine-learning-based gain tuning

The adaptive laws \(\dot\lambda, \dot T_p, \dot\phi\) are hand-derived from a Lyapunov candidate. They are safe by construction but possibly suboptimal. A small neural network could replace the analytical laws with learned ones, while a Lyapunov-style safety filter guarantees stability is preserved.

  • Architecture. A 3-input (\(s, \dot s, \int s\)), 3-output MLP — 32→16→3 hidden units, \(\tanh\) activations.
  • Training. Reinforcement learning (PPO) in simulation, with reward \(-y_1^2 - 10^{-4}U^2\).
  • Safety layer. Project the network output into the set where \(\dot V \leq 0\) holds — Control-Barrier-Function style.
  • Transfer. Domain-randomise the plant parameters during training to ease sim-to-real transfer.
📐
Direction 04 · Multi-Axis

Extension to multi-axis milling

A real milling head has 3–5 degrees of freedom interacting with chatter in each direction. The current PSSMC handles a single axis (\(y_1\)). Extending to multi-axis requires either three independent PSSMC loops (decentralised) or a single coupled controller (centralised).

  • Decentralised. Three diagonal Lyapunov functions \(V = V_x + V_y + V_z\); cross-axis coupling treated as bounded disturbance.
  • Centralised. Single \(s \in \mathbb{R}^3\); proof uses a positive-definite matrix Lyapunov function. Stronger stability guarantee, larger control authority required.
  • Spindle-tilt coupling. Add the rotational DOF \(\theta\) for high-end 5-axis machines.
  • Path tracking. Couple PSSMC chatter rejection with a higher-level G-code path tracker.
⚖️
Direction 05 · Benchmarks

Head-to-head against modern controllers

The current comparison is against classical SMC — a meaningful but minimum baseline. To properly position the PSSMC against the state of the art, three head-to-head benchmarks are planned.

  • Model-Predictive Control (MPC). A linear MPC with a 50-step horizon, \(R = 10^{-4} I\), \(Q = \text{diag}(10^3, 1)\). Measure RMS, energy, and solver latency.
  • Fractional-order SMC. Replace \(\dot e + \lambda e\) with \(D^\alpha e + \lambda e\) for \(\alpha \in [0.5, 1]\). Test whether the extra fractional degree of freedom matches PSSMC's adaptivity.
  • Super-twisting algorithm. Second-order SMC with continuous control — the closest 'cousin' to PSSMC. Compare against PSSMC on chatter, energy, and robustness to parameter mismatch.
  • Reporting. A Pareto plot of RMS vs energy across all five controllers — to make the trade-offs visible at a glance.

An Open Invitation

Each of these directions is a self-contained research project. If you are working on related problems — adaptive sliding-mode control, chatter suppression, real-time CNC, or learning-based controllers — and any of the five directions resonates, the simulation script and full Lyapunov proof are openly documented on the previous pages. The next chapter of the PSSMC is collaborative, not solo.
