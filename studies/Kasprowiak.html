Study overview

What this study derives

The foundation is the two-degree-of-freedom tool–workpiece model introduced by Kasprowiak, Parus, and Hoffmann. The model treats the cutting system as two coupled masses connected to a rigid frame by spring-damper pairs and linked through the cutting force — capturing the regenerative mechanism at the heart of chatter.

From this plant, a Predictive Smoothed Sliding Mode Controller (PSSMC) is designed step by step: the tracking error and sliding surface are defined, the equivalent and switching control components are derived, a first-order Taylor prediction compensates for actuator delay, and three adaptive parameters — λ, T, and φ — self-tune in real time based on the instantaneous surface value.

The complete seven-dimensional closed-loop system is then shown to be practically stable via a composite Lyapunov argument, guaranteeing convergence to an arbitrarily small neighbourhood of the sliding surface for any bounded disturbance.

About this study

This archive documents the complete mathematical derivation of the Predictive Smoothed Sliding Mode Controller (PSSMC) for chatter suppression in CNC turning of AISI 1045 steel.

The work is carried out by Shuma Fadhil (Reg 2024‑06‑02219) at the University of Dar es Salaam, Department of Mechanical and Industrial Engineering. It builds directly on the two-DOF cutting model published by Kasprowiak, Parus, and Hoffmann (2022).

Chatter vibration

Chatter is a self-excited machining vibration driven by a regenerative mechanism — the tool cuts a surface that already carries waviness from the previous pass. Each cut can amplify the waviness left by the last one, leading to destructive oscillations that degrade surface quality and accelerate tool wear.

Suppressing it means actively adjusting a process parameter — in this study, depth of cut — to steer the system away from the unstable regime.

Sliding mode control

Sliding mode control drives the system state onto a designer-chosen surface in state space and keeps it there. Once on the surface, closed-loop behaviour is governed entirely by the surface geometry — independent of plant parameter variations and bounded disturbances.

The predictive smoothed variant adds a Taylor-expansion preview horizon that anticipates vibration growth before it becomes severe, and replaces discontinuous switching with a smooth hyperbolic tangent to eliminate actuator chattering.

Adaptive parameters

Standard SMC uses fixed gains — a fixed gain large enough for worst-case chatter is unnecessarily aggressive during calm cutting. The PSSMC resolves this by making three parameters continuously adaptive: the sliding gain λ, the prediction horizon T, and the boundary layer thickness φ.

All three are driven by the instantaneous sliding surface value s(t). When chatter is severe, they adapt rapidly. When the system has converged, they freeze — the controller learns the appropriate gains for the current operating condition automatically.

Lyapunov stability

A Lyapunov proof provides a mathematical guarantee: for any initial condition and any bounded disturbance, the system converges to a neighbourhood of the sliding surface and remains there. This transforms an empirically good controller into a theoretically certified one.

The composite Lyapunov candidate accounts for both the sliding surface error and the parameter adaptation errors — ensuring all adaptive parameters remain bounded and the tracking error converges to zero on the sliding surface.
